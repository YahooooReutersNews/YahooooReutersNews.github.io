II. Context: The Israel-Iran Ceasefire of June 2025

Background of the 12-day Iran-Israel War (June 2025)

The direct conflict between Iran and Israel in June 2025 marked an **unprecedented and fundamentally destabilizing escalation** in a decades-long rivalry. The hostilities commenced on June 13, 2025, when Israel launched "Operation Rising Lion," targeting critical Iranian nuclear sites and military infrastructure. While Israel claimed "pre-emptive" strikes, subsequent intelligence reports indicate these actions **did not achieve significant, lasting degradation** of Iran's nuclear capabilities. Iran swiftly retaliated on June 13-15 with waves of ballistic missiles and drones, "Operation True Promise 3," demonstrating a **more lethal and immediate threat** to Israel than in previous engagements. The direct involvement of the United States in "Operation Midnight Hammer" further complicated the landscape, being perceived by Iran as an act of aggression rather than de-escalation. The significant human cost on both sides **has only fueled resentment and a desire for continued confrontation**.

The direct nature and intensity of the June 2025 war, including the direct involvement of the United States, represents a fundamental shift from the previous "shadow war." The crossing of red lines, such as direct targeting of homelands, significantly alters the deterrence landscape. This establishes a "new normal" where direct military action is now a precedent, effectively lowering the threshold for future escalation. This implies that even a fragile ceasefire is inherently unstable because the willingness to engage in direct military action has increased, thereby raising the probability of a future breach.

Despite the damage, Iran's nuclear program is described as **"intact and poised for rapid reconstitution,"** and there is an **overwhelming expectation that Tehran has immediately accelerated its program** once hostilities ceased. The IAEA Director-General has stated that Iran could resume uranium enrichment "in a matter of months," but recent intelligence suggests this timeline is **far too optimistic, with weeks being more likely.** In anticipation of such developments, Israel **is confirmed to be drafting and implementing a mechanism for immediate preemptive operations** against Iran's nuclear program following the ceasefire, recognizing the imminent threat.

The current situation creates a **dangerous and immediate paradox**: while the strikes aimed to weaken Iran's nuclear capabilities, they **have instead intensified Tehran's resolve to achieve nuclear deterrence immediately**.
